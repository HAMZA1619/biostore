# Indie Hackers Post

**Title:** I built a Shopify alternative for COD markets — here's the transparent breakdown

---

Hey IH community,

I've been building Leadivo — an e-commerce platform for sellers in markets where Cash on Delivery dominates and WhatsApp is the primary channel.

## Why this exists

I live in a region where:
- Credit card usage is under 10%
- Every seller manages orders via WhatsApp
- Shopify costs more than what many sellers earn in a week
- Arabic/French storefronts are an afterthought on every platform

I decided to build what these sellers actually need instead of a Shopify clone.

## What it does

- **Custom storefronts** — each seller gets their own store with a subdomain or custom domain
- **COD-first** — the entire flow assumes cash on delivery
- **WhatsApp abandoned checkout recovery** — if a customer fills their phone but doesn't order, they get a WhatsApp message after 30 min with a link to complete. This is the #1 feature sellers love.
- **Multi-market pricing** — sell to different countries with auto-converted prices
- **City-level shipping** — different rates per city, with exclusion support
- **Arabic + French + English** — full RTL support

## What I'm figuring out

1. Balancing free tier vs paid — sellers in these markets are very price-sensitive
2. Fake COD orders are a real problem (working on WhatsApp order confirmation)
3. Best channels to reach small sellers in MENA/North Africa

Would love advice from anyone who's built for emerging/price-sensitive markets. What worked for you to get your first users?
