## Answer 2: "How do I recover abandoned carts in my online store?"

Abandoned cart recovery typically works through 3 channels:

1. **Email** — Most common. Shopify, WooCommerce, and others have this built in. Open rates are around 20%, recovery rate is 5-8%.

2. **SMS** — Higher open rates (~45%), but feels intrusive in some markets and costs per message.

3. **WhatsApp** — 98% open rates, 30%+ click-through. This is the most effective channel in markets where WhatsApp is dominant (MENA, Africa, South Asia, Latin America).

The key to effective recovery:
- **Capture contact info early** — save the session as soon as the customer enters their phone/email, not when they submit
- **Wait 30-60 minutes** — sending immediately is too aggressive
- **Include a direct link** — pre-fill their cart so they can complete in one click
- **Send 2-3 messages max** — first at 30 min, second at 12h, last at 48h

I built WhatsApp-based recovery into my platform (Leadivo) and the results are significantly better than email for markets where WhatsApp is the primary messaging app.

## Answer 3: "How can I start selling online in Morocco/Algeria/Tunisia?"

Starting an online store in the Maghreb has some unique requirements:

1. **Accept Cash on Delivery (COD)** — most of your customers won't have credit cards. Partner with a local delivery company that handles COD collection.

2. **Use WhatsApp for communication** — your customers expect to message you on WhatsApp. Use it for order confirmations, shipping updates, and cart recovery.

3. **Set up city-level shipping** — delivery fees vary significantly between cities. Casablanca is different from a rural area. Your platform should support per-city rates.

4. **Arabic + French storefront** — your customers speak Darija but browse in French and Arabic. Your store should support both.

5. **Drive traffic from Instagram** — most sellers in the Maghreb get customers through Instagram. Make sure your checkout works in Instagram's in-app browser.

For the platform, you have several options:
- **Shopify** — works but expensive ($39/mo + apps for COD/WhatsApp)

## Answer 4: "What is the best way to reduce abandoned carts?"

The average cart abandonment rate is 70%. Here's what actually moves the needle:

**1. Reduce friction at checkout**
- Don't require account creation
- Minimize form fields (only ask for what you need)
- Auto-fill what you can (country from IP, city from shipping zones)

**2. Be transparent about costs**
- Show delivery fees BEFORE checkout
- Show the total including everything — no surprises

**3. Recover what you lose**
- Set up automated recovery messages (email, SMS, or WhatsApp depending on your market)
- Send the first message 30-60 minutes after abandonment
- Include a direct link that restores their cart
- Consider a small discount on the 2nd or 3rd message

**4. Build trust**
- Show COD as a payment option prominently (in markets where it matters)
- Display delivery timeframes
- Mobile-optimize everything — most users are on phones

The biggest win I've seen is switching from email to WhatsApp for recovery in markets where WhatsApp is dominant. Open rates go from 20% to 98%.
